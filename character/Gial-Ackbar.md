    <p><p>Gial Ackbar was a male Mon Calamari from the ocean world of Mon Cala who became one of the most celebrated military commanders in galactic history, serving across four conflicts over roughly six decades. As captain of the Mon Calamari Guard during the Clone Wars, he fought at the side of Prince Lee-Char against the Quarren separatists and the Karkarodon warlord Riff Tamson, helping preserve his people's throne with Republic and Gungan aid. Under the Galactic Empire he was enslaved along with much of his species, and after his rescue he rose through the Alliance to Restore the Republic to become commander of the rebel fleet, planning and leading the assault on the second Death Star at the Battle of Endor in 4 ABY — the engagement that made his warning of a trap into one of the most quoted moments of the war. He continued to serve the New Republic as an admiral and statesman for his homeworld before retiring, then answered General Leia Organa's call to join the Resistance against the First Order, advising the fleet through the destruction of the Hosnian system and the Battle of Starkiller Base. In 34 ABY he was killed when First Order fighters strafed the bridge of the Resistance flagship Raddus during the evacuation of D'Qar, ending a career defined by patient, meticulous tactical genius.</p></p>

    <div class="timeline"><h3>~55 BBY - Birth on Mon Cala</h3><p>Gial Ackbar is born on the ocean world of Mon Cala. Canon gives only an approximate window for his birth, roughly between 61 BBY and 52 BBY.</p><h3>~22 BBY - Captain of the Mon Calamari Guard</h3><p>Ackbar rises to captain of the Mon Calamari Guard and serves as a trusted military adviser to King Yos Kolina and his son, Prince Lee-Char.</p><h3>21 BBY - The Mon Cala civil war</h3><p>After the king's assassination, Quarren separatists backed by the Karkarodon Riff Tamson attack the Mon Calamari. Ackbar fights alongside Jedi reinforcements, Anakin Skywalker's forces and Gungan warriors sent by Padmé Amidala, protecting Lee-Char through the underwater battles.</p><h3>21 BBY - Victory and Lee-Char's coronation</h3><p>With Tamson defeated and the Quarren reconciled, Lee-Char is crowned king of a reunited Mon Cala, with Ackbar established as his leading military commander.</p><h3>~19 BBY - The Empire subjugates Mon Cala</h3><p>Following the rise of the Galactic Empire, Mon Cala is occupied and much of its population, Ackbar included, is pressed into servitude.</p><h3>~4 BBY - Service with the Rebel Alliance</h3><p>Freed from Imperial captivity, Ackbar joins the Alliance to Restore the Republic, where his tactical skill quickly earns him fleet command and the rank of admiral.</p><h3>4 ABY - Briefing at Sullust</h3><p>Ackbar presents the Alliance battle plan against the second Death Star, coordinating Han Solo's strike team on the Endor moon with the fleet's attack on the battle station.</p><h3>4 ABY - Battle of Endor</h3><p>Commanding from the Home One, Ackbar realizes the Alliance has flown into an Imperial ambush and orders the fleet to engage the Star Destroyers at point-blank range, a decision that keeps the rebels alive until the Death Star's shield falls and the station is destroyed.</p><h3>5 ABY - Campaigns for the New Republic</h3><p>Ackbar continues to lead fleets in the final campaigns against Imperial remnants as the New Republic is established, and serves his homeworld in its government.</p><h3>~28 ABY - Joining the Resistance</h3><p>Retired from New Republic service, Ackbar answers Leia Organa's summons and helps organize the Resistance against the rising First Order, becoming its senior naval adviser.</p><h3>34 ABY - Battle of Starkiller Base</h3><p>After the First Order destroys the Hosnian system, Ackbar helps plan the Resistance strike that brings down Starkiller Base.</p><h3>34 ABY - Death aboard the Raddus</h3><p>During the Resistance evacuation of D'Qar, First Order TIE fighters and Kylo Ren's TIE silencer strafe the bridge of the flagship Raddus. Ackbar is blown into space and killed, leaving Leia Organa to command the surviving fleet.</p></div>
