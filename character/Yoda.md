    <p>
    Yoda     was born in <a href="https://timeline.starwars.guide/character/Yoda?year=-300" target="_blank">896 BBY</a> and died in <a href="https://timeline.starwars.guide/character/Yoda?year=4" target="_blank">4 ABY</a>.        
    </p>

    <p><p>Yoda was a diminutive, green-skinned member of an unnamed and mysterious species who became the most revered Jedi of the modern age, serving as Grand Master of the Jedi Order and as a member of the Jedi High Council for centuries. Born around 896 BBY, he began instructing younglings roughly a century later and trained generations of Jedi over some 800 years, including Count Dooku and, indirectly, nearly every Jedi of the late Republic. Renowned less for his lightsaber prowess — though he was among the greatest duelists alive — than for his wisdom, humility and profound connection to the living Force, he guided the Order through the Naboo crisis, reluctantly led the Grand Army of the Republic as a general during the Clone Wars, and was ultimately deceived along with the rest of the Council by Darth Sidious. After Order 66 he dueled the newly declared Emperor in the Senate chamber, failed to defeat him, and accepted exile in the swamps of Dagobah, where he spent two decades learning from Qui-Gon Jinn's spirit how to retain his identity after death. In 3 ABY he trained Luke Skywalker in the ways of the Force, and in 4 ABY, at the age of 900, he died and became one with the Force — returning years later as a Force spirit to counsel Luke a final time on Ahch-To.</p></p>

    <div class="timeline"><h3>~896 BBY - Birth</h3> <p>Yoda is born, a member of a species whose name and homeworld are never revealed in canon.</p> <h3>~796 BBY - Begins training Jedi</h3> <p>About a century into his life, Yoda takes up the instruction of younglings, a vocation he will continue for roughly 800 years.</p> <h3>~232 BBY - Grand Master during the High Republic</h3> <p>By the height of the High Republic Era, Yoda serves as Grand Master of the Jedi Order, mentoring younglings and traveling the frontier alongside Jedi such as Master Zia and the initiates of Tenoo.</p> <h3>~132 BBY - Counsel after the Brendok tragedy</h3> <p>In the final years of the High Republic, Vernestra Rwoh brings Yoda word of the deaths of Indara, Torbin and Kelnacca, concealing the truth of what happened on Brendok.</p> <h3>~102 BBY - Trains Dooku</h3> <p>Yoda instructs the young Dooku, who will become one of the Order's most formidable Jedi before falling to the dark side.</p> <h3>32 BBY - The Phantom Menace</h3> <p>Yoda tests the young Anakin Skywalker and senses grave danger in the boy's fear; after Qui-Gon Jinn's death he reluctantly permits Obi-Wan Kenobi to train Anakin and recognizes that a Sith Lord has returned.</p> <h3>22 BBY - Attack of the Clones and the outbreak of war</h3> <p>Yoda leads the clone army to Geonosis to rescue the Jedi strike team, rescues Anakin and Obi-Wan from Dooku, and duels his former apprentice — the first battle of the Clone Wars.</p> <h3>22–19 BBY - The Clone Wars</h3> <p>Serving as a general of the Republic, Yoda leads campaigns such as the defense of Rugosa, survives an assassination attempt on Vjun, defends the Jedi Temple against Cad Bane's schemes, and grows increasingly troubled by the Order's role in the war.</p> <h3>~19 BBY - Journey to the living Force</h3> <p>Guided by the voice of Qui-Gon Jinn, Yoda undertakes a pilgrimage to the priestesses of Serenno's ancient world and to Moraband, confronting Darth Bane's shade and learning that he can retain his consciousness after death.</p> <h3>19 BBY - Order 66 and the duel with Sidious</h3> <p>Yoda survives the clone betrayal on Kashyyyk with the aid of Chewbacca and Tarfful, returns to Coruscant, and duels Darth Sidious in the Senate chamber. Unable to defeat the new Emperor, he withdraws.</p> <h3>19 BBY - Exile on Dagobah</h3> <p>Accepting that the Jedi have failed, Yoda goes into hiding in the swamps of Dagobah, continuing his studies with Qui-Gon's spirit and awaiting a new hope.</p> <h3>~2 BBY - Guiding Ezra Bridger</h3> <p>Through the Force, Yoda speaks to Ezra Bridger in the Jedi Temple on Lothal and later at the Temple's Force nexus, warning him of the perils of vengeance and pointing him toward Malachor.</p> <h3>3 ABY - Training Luke Skywalker</h3> <p>Luke Skywalker arrives on Dagobah at Obi-Wan's urging. Yoda tests him, reluctantly accepts him as a student, raises his X-wing from the bog, and warns him against leaving to confront Vader on Bespin.</p> <h3>4 ABY - Death and becoming one with the Force</h3> <p>At 900 years old, Yoda tells a returned Luke that Vader is his father and that another Skywalker remains, then dies and vanishes into the Force.</p> <h3>34 ABY - Return as a Force spirit</h3> <p>On Ahch-To, Yoda appears to a despairing Luke, calls down lightning on the ancient Jedi tree, and reminds him that failure is the greatest teacher — prompting Luke's final stand.</p></div>
